TRANSPOSE

Syntax: vout = <−v

The transpose operator, <−, is a unary operator that produces the transpose of a matrix, that is, the rows and columns are interchanged.

Suppose the matrix M has M rows and N columns. Then <-M has N rows and M columns, where (<-M)[i,j] = M[j,i] for i = 1,2,...,N and j = 1,2,...,M.

Example

Let 

     |  1  2  3 |
 M = |  4  5  6 |
     |  7  8  9 |
     | 10 11 12 |
then 
       |  1  4  7 10 |
 <-M = |  2  5  8 11 |
       |  3  6  9 12 |
